# Arketa to Aurea Settings Parity

Date: 2026-07-18

## Scope and status rules

This matrix cross-references every Arketa settings route observed in the signed-in, read-only audit against the current Aurea product and code. The control-level source of truth remains `ARKETA_SETTINGS_CONTROL_INVENTORY_2026-07-18.md`; the companion CSV maps every observed Arketa toggle to a domain owner, precise Aurea control, runtime consumer, evidence, status, and implementation gap. Structural controls and unlabeled DOM toggles are explicitly marked as such rather than being misrepresented as settings.

- **Implemented** means Aurea has a tenant-scoped UI and the setting affects its runtime path.
- **Partial** means Aurea has part of the management or runtime contract, but not the full observed Arketa behavior.
- **Gap** means no equivalent operator-facing capability was proven.
- Provider-specific gaps should become generalized provider capabilities, not one-off Arketa copies.

## Where Aurea is already stronger

- Workspace regional and operations settings resolve organization defaults plus location overrides field by field. Customer, commerce, and staff definitions are exact-scope foundations and still need equivalent inheritance or explicit local-copy semantics before runtime cutover.
- Immutable policy versions, optimistic concurrency, change notes, rollback metadata, and effective dates.
- Capability-based management permissions and tenant-scoped provider accounts.
- Durable delivery attempts, suppression enforcement, provider receipts, retries, and operational recovery.
- Scheduling policies are materialized onto bookings/classes so historical decisions remain explainable.
- Provider health, reconnect, reconciliation, and audit records are modeled as product operations, not hidden credentials.

## Implementation boundary

The customer, commerce, and staff routes added during this tranche are durable management foundations, not yet the sole runtime authority. Existing CRM tags/custom fields, product tax and revenue fields, guest entitlements, and payroll hourly-rate consumers must be migrated with dual-read diagnostics and historical snapshots before those domains can move from **Partial** to **Implemented**. Inbound Cal.com bookings are retained as provider truth and now carry a scheduling-policy evaluation so divergent bookings surface as exceptions instead of being silently dropped.

## Required next tranche

1. Build a versioned transactional-message rule registry: event, audience, channel, sender, template version, timing, quiet hours, locale, preview/test, enablement, and delivery purpose.
2. Materialize customer field, household, tax, revenue, offline-payment, guest-pass, and staff-compensation settings into their runtime consumers before calling those domains complete.
3. Add dedicated suppression and mailbox blocklist management with import/export, reason, expiry, revocation, audit, and permission controls.
4. Add terminology packs, FAQ collections, message macros, signup-profile schemas, and public business-profile sections as reusable versioned content capabilities.
5. Add generalized provider families for marketplaces, access control, marketing sync, video meetings, and fitness display integrations with lifecycle and reconciliation.

## Evidence limits

- The Arketa audit proves only controls visible to this signed-in plan, role, tenant, and feature set.
- No Arketa values, settings, integrations, or records were saved or changed.
- A route marked Implemented here still requires browser acceptance testing against an initialized local Aurea database before release.
